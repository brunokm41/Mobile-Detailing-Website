The Process

What's Included in Every Ceramic Coating

Ceramic coating is not a product you apply over a dirty or scratched surface — preparation is everything. Every coating job starts with thorough decontamination and paint correction to ensure the ceramic bonds to flawless paint.

We require the vehicle for a minimum of 2 days. This allows proper curing time and ensures we never rush a job that's meant to last years.

  • Full wash & iron decontamination
  • Clay bar treatment
  • Paint thickness measurement
  • Single-stage paint correction (swirl & scratch removal)
  • Panel wipe-down with IPA solution
  • Ceramic coating applied panel by panel
  • Levelling & flash time per panel
  • Infrared curing (where applicable)
  • Full inspection under lighting
  • Coating maintenance guide provided

Why Ceramic Coating

The Benefits

Far beyond a wax or sealant — a permanent layer of protection for your paint.

01
Permanent Bonding

Unlike wax or sealants that sit on top of your paint, ceramic coating chemically bonds to the surface — it can't be washed off and lasts years.

02
Hydrophobic Effect

Water beads and rolls off the surface, taking dirt and contaminants with it. Your car stays cleaner for longer and is far easier to wash.

03
UV Protection

New Zealand's UV is harsh. Ceramic coating blocks UV rays that cause paint oxidation and fading — keeping your colour vibrant for years.

04
Chemical Resistance

Bird droppings, tree sap and road grime are highly acidic. The ceramic layer acts as a sacrificial shield protecting your paint.

05
Enhanced Gloss

The coating amplifies your paint's depth and clarity, producing a wet-look gloss noticeably deeper and richer than an uncoated vehicle.

06
Protects Resale Value

A vehicle with documented ceramic coating is more appealing to buyers — it signals the car has been cared for at the highest level.
